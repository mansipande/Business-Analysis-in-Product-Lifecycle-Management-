Business Analysis in Product Lifecycle Management

📌 Overview
This project explores the critical role of **Business Analysis (BA)** within **Product Lifecycle Management (PLM)**. While PLM provides the structural framework for managing a product’s journey from ideation to retirement, business analysis ensures that decisions are evidence-based, strategically aligned, and responsive to market needs.  

The document highlights how business analysts act as the bridge between stakeholders, technical teams, and customers, ensuring that products evolve efficiently and sustainably.

🎯 Objectives
- Explain the importance of business analysis in guiding PLM processes.
- Demonstrate how BA supports decision-making, risk management, and stakeholder alignment.
- Explore modelling techniques (BPMN, UML, VSM) for process optimisation.
- Present key business analysis techniques (SWOT, user stories, use cases, prototyping, KPIs).
- Identify challenges faced by analysts in PLM environments.
- Evaluate the impact of effective business analysis on product success.

🏗️ Key Sections
1. **Introduction** – Context of PLM and the role of business analysis.  
2. **Product Lifecycle Management** – Definition, stages, and strategic importance.  
3. **Business Analysis in PLM** – How BA strengthens PLM through requirements, risk evaluation, and stakeholder communication.  
4. **Process Modelling & Optimisation** – Use of BPMN, UML, and VSM to streamline workflows.  
5. **Business Analysis Techniques** – SWOT, user stories, use cases, prototyping, wireframing, KPIs.  
6. **Challenges in BA for PLM** – Conflicting expectations, data quality issues, time pressure, and organisational barriers.  
7. **Impact of BA in PLM** – Improved clarity, collaboration, decision-making, and risk reduction.  
8. **Conclusion** – BA as a stabilising force that ensures products evolve strategically and sustainably.  

🔑 Key Insights
- **Business Analysts = Compass + Bridge**: They provide direction and connect diverse teams.  
- **Evidence-Based Decisions**: BA ensures product strategies are grounded in validated needs.  
- **Continuous Improvement**: Performance data and feedback drive ongoing product optimisation.  
- **Risk Reduction**: Analysts anticipate challenges before they escalate.  
- **Strategic Impact**: Strong BA practices sharpen innovation and sustain competitiveness.  

📚 Techniques Covered
- **SWOT Analysis** – Strategic evaluation of strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats.  
- **User Stories & Use Cases** – Translating business needs into actionable requirements.  
- **Prototyping & Wireframing** – Early validation of product concepts.  
- **Data Analysis & KPIs** – Measuring product performance and guiding improvements.  
- **Process Modelling (BPMN, UML, VSM)** – Visualising workflows and optimising efficiency.  

🚀 Benefits of Effective Business Analysis in PLM
- Clearer requirements and reduced rework.  
- Stronger stakeholder alignment and collaboration.  
- Faster, evidence-based decision-making.  
- Improved product quality and customer satisfaction.  
- Long-term sustainability and competitive advantage.
